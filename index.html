Q1 What led you to consider our school for your child's education? 저희 학교를 고려하게 된 계기는 무엇인가요?

I studied in Canada for a period, and that gave me a real trust in Canada as a place where kids can grow up safely and well.

When I started looking for schools, Canada felt like the natural choice.

A trusted friend who had lived in Canada recommended Appleby — she said if she had stayed, she would have sent her own child here. That meant a lot to me, so honestly Appleby was already in my heart before I even did my research.

When I looked into it more, Oakville's safe and beautiful environment really put me at ease as a parent.

Appleby's mission — developing leaders of strong character — matched exactly what we believe in as a family.

The structure of Arts, Athletics, and Service every single term, and students from over 45 countries… I felt this was truly the right place for my child.

잠깐 캐나다에서 공부했던 경험이 있어 캐나다는 안전한 환경에서 성장할 수 있는 국가라는 믿음이 있었음. 그래서 아이를 위한 학교를 캐나다에서 알아봤음.

캐나다 지인이 Appleby College를 적극 추천했음. 본인이 캐나다에 계속 있었으면 자녀를 보내고 싶다고 했고, 그 말이 큰 울림이 되었음.

이후 알아보던 중, 오크빌의 안전하고 아름다운 환경이 부모로서 마음이 놓였고, "강한 인성을 가진 리더"를 키운다는 미션이 우리 교육관과 일치한다고 느꼈음.

매 학기 Arts·Athletics·Service를 균형 있게 참여하는 구조와, 45개국 이상의 학생이 함께하는 보딩 커뮤니티가 이 아이에게 최적의 환경이라고 생각함.

Q2 Can you share some specific goals you have for your child's education and personal development? 자녀의 교육과 성장에 대해 어떤 목표를 갖고 계신가요?

More than anything, I want her to really know herself — to understand her own unique strengths and qualities, and be able to express them.

And to use those strengths to make a real difference for others and for society.

Appleby's mission — developing leaders of strong character who contribute to their communities and the world — is exactly what we hope for our daughter too.

자기 자신을 잘 아는 사람. 자신의 고유한 성향과 강점을 인지하고 발현할 수 있는 사람.

그 강점을 기반으로 타인과 사회에 긍정적인 영향을 끼치는 사람으로 자라길 바람.

Appleby의 미션인 "강한 인성을 가진 리더로서 지역사회와 세계에 긍정적인 기여를 하는 사람"과 우리가 바라는 아이의 모습이 깊이 맞닿아 있다고 느낌.

Q3 How would you describe your child's learning style and any specific educational needs they may have? 자녀의 학습 스타일과 특별한 교육적 필요가 있다면 설명해주세요.

She's very much an internally motivated learner — external pressure doesn't work, but when something genuinely interests her, she goes really deep.

She learns best through direct experience and feeling things, not memorizing from a textbook.

She has a natural ability to connect what she's learned across different areas — she thinks in an integrated, liberal arts way.

And one thing that's always been consistent is her need to express herself — as a child it was through performing arts, and now it shows through fashion and makeup. The form has changed, but the instinct to express who she is has always been there.

내적 동기 부여형, 경험형, 감각형. 교과서 암기보다 직접 경험하고 느끼며 배우는 스타일.

흥미와 내적 동기가 생기면 탐구의 깊이가 남다름 — 스스로 파고드는 힘이 강한 아이.

배운 것들을 서로 연결하는 능력이 있어, 다양한 분야를 통합적으로 사고하는 리버럴 아츠적 접근과 잘 맞음.

어릴 때부터 자기표현에 대한 본능이 강한 아이 — 어릴 때는 공연 예술로, 지금은 패션과 화장으로 표현 방식은 바뀌었지만 자신을 표현하고자 하는 본능은 변하지 않았음.

Q4 What aspects of our school's curriculum or teaching philosophy align with your educational values? 학교의 커리큘럼이나 교육 철학 중 공감하는 부분이 있나요?

Above all, Appleby's philosophy of discovering and nurturing each student's individual passion resonates so deeply with how I've tried to raise my daughter.

Finding a school that shares this exact belief felt genuinely exciting and reassuring.

The emphasis on inquiry-based and experiential learning is a perfect fit for how she actually learns — she needs to experience and explore, not just memorize.

The availability of specialized tracks like the Global Leadership Diploma, so students can go deep in their own direction, is also very appealing.

"학생 개개인의 열정을 발견하고 지원한다"는 철학이 내가 아이를 양육해온 철학과 너무나 잘 맞닿아 있음. 그 철학을 가진 학교를 만나 반갑고 감사했음.

탐구형·경험형 학습을 강조하는 Appleby의 교육 철학이 우리 아이의 학습 스타일과 잘 맞는다고 생각함.

Global Leadership 디플로마처럼 학생이 자신의 방향성에 맞게 전문화할 수 있는 트랙이 있다는 점도 매력적임.

Q5 Can you discuss your expectations for communication and involvement with teachers and school staff? 교사 및 학교 스태프와의 소통에 대해 어떤 기대를 갖고 계신가요?

There's a saying I really believe in — "it takes a village to raise a child."

Trusted adults and peers beyond parents are so important for a child's growth.

I see the relationship between school and parents as a real partnership — built on shared values and frequent, open communication.

I'm really looking forward to working closely and collaboratively with Appleby's teachers and staff.

"한 아이를 키우려면 온 마을이 필요하다." 부모 이외의 좋은 어른들과 또래 관계가 성장의 핵심이라고 믿음.

학교와 부모가 공통된 교육관으로 자주 소통하는 파트너 관계라고 생각함. 따라서 자주 소통하고 협력해야 함.

Q6 How do you envision your child benefiting from the extracurricular activities offered at our school? 학교의 과외 활동이 자녀에게 어떤 도움이 될 거라고 생각하시나요?

I really see Appleby's co-curricular programs as a space to draw out what's already inside her — her inner passions and identity.

She has always had a strong instinct for self-expression — as a child through vocal music, drama, and musical theatre, and now through fashion and makeup.

Appleby's Vocal, Drama, and Performing Arts programs mean she can reconnect with those roots.

The broadcasting and media club is a very natural continuation of her elementary school broadcasting experience.

Getting to skate again on Appleby's campus is also something she's genuinely excited about.

The structure of Athletics, Arts, and Service every term is exactly right for a child who has always grown by exploring many different things.

Appleby의 과외 활동은 이 아이 안에 있는 것을 이끌어낼 수 있는 최적의 공간이 될 것이라 기대함.

어릴 때는 성악·드라마·뮤지컬로, 지금은 패션과 화장으로 — 표현의 형태는 달라졌지만 그 본능은 늘 있어왔음.

Vocal, Drama, Performing Arts 프로그램을 통해 그 뿌리와 다시 연결될 수 있을 것이라 기대됨. 방송·미디어 클럽도 자연스러운 연장선.

어릴 때 즐겼던 스케이팅을 캠퍼스에서 다시 시작할 수 있다는 점도 큰 기대 요소임.

매 학기 Athletics·Arts·Service를 균형 있게 참여하는 구조가, 다양한 경험을 통해 자신을 발견해온 이 아이에게 가장 잘 맞는 환경이라고 생각함.

Q7 Are there any specific concerns or challenges you anticipate your child may face in our school environment? 자녀가 학교 환경에서 어떤 어려움을 겪을 수 있다고 예상하시나요?

The biggest challenge will probably be self-management at the beginning — this is her first time living away from us.

She's always had parental support close by, so doing everything independently might feel unfamiliar at first.

But she's the kind of person who gets excited about new environments rather than anxious, so I believe she'll adapt well.

Having caring teachers around during that initial adjustment period will make a huge difference.

부모와 떨어져 처음 경험하는 보딩 환경이기에, 초반 자기 관리가 가장 큰 도전이 될 것으로 예상.

하지만 새로운 환경에 대한 기대와 적응력이 있는 아이라 잘 해낼 것이라 믿음. 초반 적응 과정에서 선생님들의 관심이 큰 힘이 될 것.

Q8 How do you support your child's education at home, and what role do you see for parents in our school community? 가정에서 어떻게 교육을 지원하시나요? 학부모로서 어떤 역할을 하고 싶으신가요?

Everything starts at home — that's what I truly believe.

We've taught her about social life and responsibility within our extended family — navigating conflicts, following rules, being part of a community.

We also taught her the importance of contributing and serving — every New Year's Day, each child is given a role, so she naturally learns what it means to give back.

When she was younger, I focused on exposing her to lots of different experiences — vocal music, musical theatre, art, writing — to help her find her own strengths.

Now that she's a teenager, my role has shifted — rather than stepping in directly, I connect her with the right trusted adults who can guide her best.

As a parent, I see my role as keeping the big picture in mind and watching over her, while she grows through her own relationships and experiences.

In terms of my role in the school community, I'd love to be an active and present parent — genuinely partnering with teachers and staff, and supporting whatever the school needs from parents to help her thrive.

가정교육이 모든 것의 기반이라고 생각함. 대가족 공동체 안에서 갈등과 규율을 배우며 사회화를 가르쳐왔음.

매년 1월 1일 아이들에게 역할을 부여하여, 가족이라는 작은 사회 안에서 섬기는 자세를 자연스럽게 익히게 함.

어린 시절엔 성악·뮤지컬·미술·글쓰기 등 다양한 경험을 통해 재능을 찾도록 지원. 틴에이저가 된 이후엔 직접 개입보다 잘 이끌어줄 수 있는 어른을 연결해주는 역할로 전환.

학교 커뮤니티에서는 선생님·스태프와 진정한 파트너로서, 아이의 성장을 위해 적극적으로 협력하는 역할을 하고자 함.

Q9 Can you share any previous experiences you've had with private schools and what you appreciated about those experiences? 이전 사립학교 경험이 있으시다면, 어떤 점이 좋았나요?

Both of my daughter's elementary and middle schools were private, while my other child went to public school — so I've directly compared the two.

What I'm most grateful for is this real sense that school and parents are raising the child together.

Three things I've appreciated most about private schools:

1. Consistent communication and guidelines based on the school's philosophy — it gives you a clear picture of the direction your child is growing in.

2. High level of individual attention from teachers — not just accommodating the child, but genuinely working together to find the best direction for her growth.

3. Relationships that continue even after graduation — teachers who walk alongside a child's growth over many years.

The variety of extracurricular activities also helped us discover her strengths — through the broadcasting club, vocal competitions, and speed skating, we found that she loves speaking, writing, singing, and skating.

아이 초·중학교 모두 사립, 다른 자녀는 공립 — 직접 비교 경험 있음.

가장 감사한 점은 학교와 부모가 파트너가 되어 한 아이를 함께 성장시킨 경험과 믿음.

사립의 장점 세 가지: 1) 학교 철학 기반의 일관된 소통과 가이드라인. 2) 선생님들의 학생 개개인에 대한 높은 관심도 — 맞춰주는 게 아니라 함께 성장 방향을 잡아줌. 3) 졸업 후에도 관계 지속 — 긴 호흡으로 함께함.

방송부, 성악 경연대회, 스피드스케이팅 등을 통해 아이가 말하기·글쓰기·노래·스케이팅을 좋아한다는 것을 알게 되었음.

Q10 How do you handle academic or behavioral challenges with your child, and what role do you see the school playing in such situations? 학업이나 행동 문제가 생겼을 때 어떻게 대응하시나요? 학교의 역할은 어떻게 보시나요?

Whenever there's an issue, my first instinct is to look beneath the surface — is this really an academic problem, or is something going on emotionally?

If it's emotional, I create space for her to talk it through with me or another trusted adult. Once that's addressed, we work on the academic side with a teacher who's a good fit for her.

For behavioral issues, I try to understand where she's coming from emotionally, but I'm clear that feelings can't become attitude that affects others.

I'm really confident in Appleby's guidance and counselling system — the fact that students feel safe to openly express what they're going through is actually one of the reasons we chose this school.

When school and parents work together in the same direction, that's when a child grows the most.

우선 표면적인 문제인지 아니면 마음의 문제인지 살펴봄. 마음의 문제일 경우 부모 및 믿는 어른과의 대화를 통해 근본 원인을 이야기함.

행동 문제의 경우 마음은 이해하지만 감정이 타인에게 영향을 미치는 태도가 되지 않도록 지도함.

Appleby의 강력한 guidance 시스템과 상담 문화를 신뢰하며, 아이가 자신의 감정을 자유롭게 표현할 수 있는 환경이 이 학교를 선택한 이유 중 하나임.

학교와 부모가 같은 방향을 보며 협력할 때 아이가 가장 잘 성장할 수 있다고 믿음.

Q11 Are there any family traditions or cultural considerations that you would like us to be aware of? 자녀 교육과 관련해 고려해야 할 가족 전통이나 문화적 요소가 있나요?

Nothing particularly special to flag, but we're a large, music-loving family — over 30 of us.

Every New Year's Day, we hold a family concert where grandparents, parents, and relatives all perform together — so being on a stage and expressing herself has just been a normal part of her life since she was very young.

We host Chuseok and New Year's Day at our home every year, and each child is given a role — they naturally learn what it means to contribute within a community.

Through that, she's learned to appreciate differences and work together with all kinds of people.

I hope she carries her Korean identity with her while also connecting with people from all kinds of backgrounds.

음악을 사랑하는 30명이 넘는 대가족임. 매년 1월 1일 가족 음악회를 열어 조부모·부모·친척 모두가 각자의 재능을 펼침 — 이 아이에게 무대와 표현은 어릴 때부터 일상이었음.

설날·추석은 매년 우리 집에서 직접 챙기며 아이들에게 각자 역할을 부여 — 공동체 안에서 자기 역할을 배우게 함.

한국인 정체성을 유지하면서도 다양한 배경의 사람들과 어울릴 수 있는 사람으로 자라길 바람.

Q12 How do you encourage a balance between academics and extracurricular activities in your child's life? 학업과 과외 활동의 균형을 어떻게 잡아주시나요?

Extracurricular activities aren't a break from academics — they're the foundation, the place where she finds her identity and her color.

When she was younger, the focus was on exploring broadly — trying lots of different things to figure out what she loves.

What I've seen is that when she finds something she's genuinely passionate about, that naturally becomes the motivation to go deeper academically.

Appleby's structure — Athletics, Arts, and Service every term — matches this idea of broad exploration first, then gradually going deeper.

We believe in taking responsibility for what you commit to, but ultimately the goal is to find her own unique voice through a variety of experiences.

과외 활동은 정체성과 삶의 풍요로움의 기반 — 자신의 색깔을 찾기 위해 매우 중요하다고 생각함. 그 기반 위에서 학업이 이어져야 함.

탐색을 통해 발견한 관심과 재능이 학업의 동기부여로 이어진다고 믿음.

Appleby의 매 학기 Athletics·Arts·Service 구조가, 넓은 탐색 후 점차 깊이를 더해가는 이 아이의 성장 방식과 잘 맞는다고 생각함.

Q13 What role do you believe technology should play in your child's education, and how do you manage screen time at home? 기술과 스크린 타임에 대해 어떤 생각을 갖고 계신가요?

Technology, when used well, can really help kids learn more efficiently — self-management tools, reviewing mistakes, these are so much easier with technology.

But relying too heavily on AI at too young an age can get in the way of developing independent thinking and finding your own voice.

My hope is that during the teenage years, she learns to use technology as a tool without becoming overly dependent on it.

In terms of screen time — she got her first smartphone in fifth grade, and we've managed it consistently ever since. Age-appropriate content restrictions, a set daily limit, and at bedtime everything except essential apps is blocked.

기술은 막는 게 아니라 잘 활용해야 하는 것. 자기 관리 도구 및 오답 정리 등에서 기술의 도움을 적극 활용할 수 있음.

다만 너무 이른 시기에 AI에 의존하면 생각하는 힘과 자기만의 색깔을 찾는 데 허들이 될 수 있어, 청소년기에는 과의존하지 않도록 지도하고 있음.

5학년 첫 스마트폰 이후 스크린 타임을 일관되게 관리 중 — 연령 맞춤 콘텐츠 제한, 일일 사용 시간 설정, 취침 시 필수 앱 외 차단.

Q14 Can you discuss your expectations regarding homework and your involvement in your child's assignments? 숙제와 학업 지원에 대해 어떤 기대를 갖고 계신가요?

Homework is essential — it's how you build study habits and make knowledge truly your own.

One thing I've always emphasized is consistency — rather than cramming, I've encouraged her to break things down and do a little bit every day.

My approach is to encourage her to work through things independently first. When she hits a real wall, the best thing is to use the school's teachers and guidance system rather than having me step in and solve it.

My role as a parent is checking in on how she's doing overall and staying in close communication with the school.

숙제는 공부 습관을 형성하고 지식을 내면화하는 데 꼭 필요하다고 생각함.

몰아서 하기보다 매일 조금씩 나눠서 꾸준히 하는 습관을 들이도록 지도해왔음.

어려움이 생길 경우 학교의 선생님과 guidance 시스템을 적극적으로 활용하는 것이 가장 바람직하며, 부모는 아이의 상태를 살피고 학교와 소통하는 역할을 하고자 함.

Q15 How do you address and promote diversity and inclusion within your family, and how do you hope the school will contribute to this? 가정에서 다양성과 포용을 어떻게 가르치시나요? 학교에 기대하는 바는요?

Diversity isn't something you learn from a textbook — it's something you live.

It starts with knowing yourself, and then recognizing and respecting that others are different.

She's grown up in a big extended family — over 30 people — with siblings and cousins of different personalities and genders. Through big and small conflicts and reconciliations, she's naturally learned how to accept differences and find common ground.

Our extended family includes people from all over Korea, as well as family members living in the US, Canada, and the UK, with different nationalities and backgrounds — so diversity has genuinely just been part of everyday life.

I'm really hoping that at Appleby, she gets to expand that further — meeting people from even more diverse backgrounds and broadening her perspective.

I believe that kind of environment will make her own identity stronger, while also helping her genuinely appreciate others.

다양성은 개념이 아니라 일상에서 배우는 것이라고 생각함. 자신을 잘 알고, 타인과의 다름을 인정하는 것에서부터 시작된다고 믿음.

형제자매가 많은 대가족 안에서 성별과 성향의 차이를 자연스럽게 경험해왔음. 사촌·동생들과의 갈등과 화해 속에서 다름을 인정하고 맞춰나가는 법을 몸으로 익혀왔음.

대가족 안에 미국·캐나다·영국에 사는 가족, 다른 국적과 인종을 가진 가족도 있어 다양성이 이미 일상 안에 있음.

보딩스쿨에서 더 다양한 배경의 친구들을 만나며 관점을 넓혀나가길 기대함. 그 환경 속에서 본인의 정체성은 더욱 단단해지고, 타인을 진심으로 인정하는 사람으로 성장하길 바람.

Q17 How do you handle and support your child during times of transition or change, such as starting at a new school? 전학 등 변화와 전환 시기에 아이를 어떻게 지원하시나요?

I really believe that when parents genuinely trust a school and express that openly in front of their child, it makes a huge difference in how she adapts.

Every school she's attended has been a place I trusted completely — her current middle school is one I had my eye on since she was a toddler. When she sees that her parents truly believe in where she's being sent, she starts with security and excitement rather than anxiety.

That said, transitions are still hard, and it's okay for things to feel unfamiliar at first. What helps most isn't solving the problem for her — it's listening and helping her feel settled emotionally.

Going through those moments repeatedly is actually what builds resilience.

She's the kind of person who faces change with more excitement than fear, so I have every confidence she'll handle this transition well.

부모가 학교에 대한 신뢰를 갖고 아이 앞에서 충분히 표현하는 것이 아이의 적응에 매우 중요하다고 생각함.

지금까지 보낸 학교는 모두 부모인 내가 무한 신뢰하는 곳이었고, 아이의 현재 중학교는 토들러 시절부터 보내고자 정했던 곳임.

전환기에 힘들어할 때, 건건이 해결해주기보다 이야기를 들어주고 마음을 안정시켜주는 것이 가장 중요하다고 생각함. 그러한 반복되는 경험이 아이를 성장시킴.

변화 앞에서 불안해하기보다 기대가 큰 아이라 이번 전환도 잘 해낼 것이라 믿음.

Q18 What are your thoughts on parent involvement in school events and activities, and how do you envision your participation? 학교 행사 참여에 대해 어떤 생각이신가요?

School and family are partners in raising a child together — so being involved feels natural and important to me.

School is one of the most important windows into who my daughter really is.

I've never missed a school event until now, and even with the distance, I fully intend to be there in person for the important ones at Appleby.

It matters to her that she feels her parents are paying attention. And for me, staying connected to the school community is part of how I support her growth.

학교와 가정은 아이의 성장을 위한 동반 파트너임. 학교 활동에 참여하는 것은 한 아이를 키우기 위해 중요한 요소라고 생각함.

지금까지 학교 행사에 빠짐없이 참여해왔음. 보딩스쿨 진학 후에도 거리에 관계없이 중요한 행사엔 직접 참여할 생각.

아이가 부모의 관심을 느끼는 것도 중요하고, 부모 입장에서도 중요한 성장 파트너인 학교와 협력하는 것이 아이의 성장을 위해 매우 중요하다고 생각함.

Q19 How do you foster a love for learning and curiosity in your child outside of the formal school environment? 학교 밖에서 배움에 대한 호기심을 어떻게 길러주시나요?

Loving learning is something you carry with you for life — I truly believe that.

For her, I've tried to nurture a global perspective through travel to different countries and direct experiences in the world.

Beyond books and museums, I've tried to help her see that learning happens everywhere — in nature, in conversations with grandparents, in the stories of our family history.

Personally, I love learning new things and adapting — and I think a lot of that comes from my father, who has never stopped learning even now. Watching him explore new things throughout my childhood just naturally rubbed off on me.

The most powerful thing I can do is model it — to live a life where I'm genuinely curious and growing, and let her see that.

배움을 사랑하는 것은 평생 가져가야 할 덕목이라고 생각함.

다양한 나라 여행과 직접 경험을 통해 글로벌한 시각을 자연스럽게 길러주려 노력해왔음. 책과 박물관은 물론, 자연 속 경험과 조부모님과의 대화를 통해 배움이 교실 밖에도 있다는 것을 느끼게 해주고자 했음.

부모로서 나 스스로도 배움의 끈을 놓지 않으시는 아버지께 큰 영향을 받았음. 스스로 배우고 성장하는 삶을 직접 보여주려고 노력하고 있음.

Being directly involved in school life is the best way to really understand the community and stay connected.
